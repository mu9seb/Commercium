Time Dilation

Life, if time were slowed.
The afterimage photographed above is the result of a long & steady exposure shot. Specifically, when the shutter speed of a camera is greater than a value of about 1/15th of a second, the image captured will contain motion blur. This extent of this effect parallels the shutter speed. From this, you might infer that this photo were taken at a shutter speed of about 6 seconds. Long enough for passing vehicles to come into frame and exit in one snap of our camera's aperature, or, perhaps, a blink of an eye.
